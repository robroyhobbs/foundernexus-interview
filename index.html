Starting point

The first 30 days

A draft to shape together — not a finished plan.

Week 1 — Listen
Understand the room before trying to fill it
  • Sit in on 2-3 events. Talk to 5-8 members. Hear the language they actually use.
  • Audit every touchpoint: website, application, emails, event descriptions.
  • Deep conversation with leadership on origin story, vision, non-negotiables.
  • Deliverables: Member Language Bank, Current State Audit, Member Journey Map
Week 2 — Architect
Define the position
  • Core messaging framework: the Warrant Story in 1-line, 3-line, full paragraph.
  • Competitive differentiation map — what we say vs. what they say.
  • Signal quality metrics defined — so we're measuring from day one.
  • Deliverables: Positioning Doc, Messaging Framework, Dashboard Spec
Week 3 — Build
Create the tools
  • Outbound email framework, event templates, chapter messaging playbook.
  • "Why FounderNexus" one-pager for member referrals.
  • Partner event positioning guidelines — value-first, not sponsored-feeling.
  • Deliverables: Chapter Kit, Outbound Framework, Partner Guidelines
Week 4 — Test
Small bets, measured carefully
  • Launch 2-3 messaging tests in controlled channels.
  • Pilot the chapter playbook with one chapter leader.
  • Start the content cadence — organization-led, not personality-led.
  • Deliverables: Test Results, Chapter Pilot Report, 60-Day Proposal
  • Does this pacing feel right?
  • What existing work should I build on rather than starting from scratch?
  • Are there constraints I should know about — budget, team, partnerships in flight?
If we move forward

Decisions to make together

  • Lead angle Which positioning idea leads? Warrant model, selectivity, or outcomes? This determines the messaging architecture.
  • Member access Can I sit in on events and talk to members in week one? The language bank is the foundation.
  • Measurement alignment Success = quality of inbound, not volume. Let's define the specific metrics early.
  • Partner positioning How should the partner program be messaged so it enhances the member experience rather than feeling transactional?
  • Chapter timeline How fast are new chapters opening? This determines whether the playbook is a month-one or month-three deliverable.
  • Partnership economics I want to understand the warrant model as a partner, not just as a marketer. What does the timeline look like realistically?

The best version of this role arrives with the right questions, listens hard, and builds from what they hear. That's what I do.
